Privacy Policy — Meeting Recorder & Task Extractor

Last updated: July 9, 2026

Meeting Recorder & Task Extractor ("the extension") records audio from your active Google Meet tab, transcribes it, and generates a summary with action items. This policy explains exactly what data the extension handles and where it goes.

What data the extension processes

The extension processes: (1) audio from the Google Meet tab you choose to record, and optionally your microphone if you enable that setting; (2) participant names you enter manually or that are detected from the Meet page; (3) the meeting title and URL; (4) transcripts and summaries generated from the audio.

Where data is sent

Recorded audio is sent to the AI provider you select and configure with your own API key — either Groq (api.groq.com) or OpenAI (api.openai.com) — solely to produce a transcript and summary. Their handling of that data is governed by their own policies (Groq, OpenAI). The extension has no servers of its own. No audio, transcript, name, key, or usage data is ever sent to the developer or any other third party.

Where data is stored

Transcripts, summaries, participant rosters, settings, and your API key are stored locally in your browser (chrome.storage and IndexedDB) on your device only. Audio chunks are stored temporarily during a recording and deleted once transcription completes. You can delete all stored data by removing the extension.

What the extension does not do

It does not collect analytics or telemetry, does not use cookies, does not track browsing, does not sell or share any data, and does not record anything unless you explicitly press "Start recording".

Your responsibility: recording consent

Laws in many jurisdictions require the consent of some or all participants before recording a conversation. You are responsible for obtaining any required consent before recording a meeting.
